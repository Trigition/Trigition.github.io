Flocking

How Simple Rules Lead to Complex Behavior

Complex Adaptive Systems can also be used to demonstrate how behaviors in animals arise. In this case we look at bird flocking. In the case of bird delta formations, it was commonly believed that the bird at the front was the "leader". However this meant there was some form of hierarchical social structure that was not seen in many bird flocks.

With the lack of evidence for leadership in bird flocks, it was proposed that birds may only be concerned about their distance to other birds and their heading.

The Rules

  • A bird must maintain its distance to its neighbor to the best of its ability.
  • A bird must adjust its heading towards the rest of the flock (by averaging)
