Traffic Modelling

A Practical Use of Complex Systems

Everybody hates traffic Citation Needed. But this annoying pattern can be modelled using complex systems. By representing cars and traffic lights as their own entities, we can show how the environment (road topology) and timing can cause immensly different outcomes.

The Cars

The cars follow rules that every (responsible) driver will follow. They will not collide with another car. They will only pass an intersection when the light is green. This is the only behavior governing the cars.

The Lights

The traffic lights act a little smarter than a timed traffic light. They change their status according to the number of cars waiting in line. A single traffic light is not concerned with the status of neighboring intersections. A traffic light is only considering the cars waiting on its own signal.

Powerloss

This model include the option to cause a power-loss to the network, forcing everyone to treat the intersection as a 4-way stop. With this option enabled, we can see how the average wait time for every car starts to climb.
